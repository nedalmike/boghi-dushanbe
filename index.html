01 — Executive Summary

The Opportunity of a Generation

In the heart of Dushanbe, 30 hectares of industrial land present a once-in-a-century opportunity to reshape the capital of Tajikistan. Boghi Dushanbe envisions the transformation of this underutilized site — currently occupied by aging thermal power and industrial infrastructure — into a vibrant, mixed-use district that serves as the new center of civic, commercial, and cultural life for a nation on the rise.

The Context

Dushanbe's population of 1.07 million is growing at 2.73% annually, generating intense demand for housing, employment, and urban amenities that the current building stock cannot meet. The city is expanding its boundaries to 25,000 hectares by 2040, yet the most valuable intervention lies not at the periphery but in the center — where infrastructure already exists and where transformation carries the greatest symbolic and economic weight.

International institutions including the World Bank (Dushanbe Sustainable Urban Development Project) and the EBRD (Tajikistan Country Strategy 2025–2030, with green cities as a priority) are already investing in Dushanbe's modernization, creating a favorable environment for large-scale urban development.

The Vision

Boghi Dushanbe is not merely a real estate development. It is a statement of national ambition: that Tajikistan can build world-class urbanism rooted in its own culture, climate, and values. The project proposes a phased, public-private approach to delivering approximately 12,000–15,000 residential units, 200,000–300,000 sqm of commercial space, destination retail, a landmark cultural center, a luxury hotel, and extensive public parks — all within a walkable, green, and architecturally coherent district.

The name itself — "Boghi Dushanbe," meaning "Garden of Dushanbe" — signals the intent: to create an urban oasis where landscape, water, mountain views, and contemporary Tajik architecture converge to produce a place of enduring civic pride.

Approach: This document presents a conceptual framework for consideration. All figures, areas, and projections are indicative and subject to detailed feasibility studies, environmental assessments, infrastructure analysis, and legal review. The intent is to demonstrate the potential of the site and provide a foundation for informed government decision-making.

03 — Vision

Why This Site. Why Now. Why This Matters.

A Rare Asset

Thirty hectares of contiguous land in central Dushanbe is an extraordinary asset. In most capital cities worldwide, sites of this scale simply do not become available. The Luchob-area industrial site — located near the city's botanical gardens, with potential proximity to the Varzob River corridor, and within reach of Dushanbe's institutional and commercial core — is a site of genuine strategic significance.

Its current use as aging industrial and thermal power infrastructure represents a legacy of Soviet-era planning that, while historically important, no longer reflects the needs or aspirations of modern Dushanbe. As power generation shifts to newer facilities and the city center demands higher and better uses, the rationale for transformation becomes compelling.

A City in Transformation

Dushanbe is not standing still. The planned RMJM Dushanbe Tower — envisioned as a supertall landmark — signals the city's readiness to join the ranks of modern global capitals. The World Bank's Dushanbe Sustainable Urban Development Project and the EBRD's 2025–2030 country strategy (which explicitly includes green city development) demonstrate international confidence in Tajikistan's urban future.

The city's expansion plan to 25,000 hectares by 2040 acknowledges the demographic reality: a population growing at 2.73% annually, a real estate market appreciating at 4.69% per year, and a chronic housing shortage that affects families at every income level.

A Symbol of National Progress

The transformation of an industrial site into a world-class mixed-use district is more than an urban planning exercise. It is a statement about national identity and direction. It says that Tajikistan values its people's quality of life, that it embraces modern standards of sustainability and design, and that it can attract international expertise and investment while remaining rooted in its own cultural traditions.

Boghi Dushanbe would join a lineage of transformative urban projects — from London's King's Cross to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon, from Singapore's Marina Bay to Astana's new city center — where the deliberate reimagining of underused urban land catalyzed broader national progress.

The Human Case

Behind every statistic is a Dushanbe family seeking a dignified home, a young professional searching for career opportunities close to home, a child who deserves safe parks and good schools. The housing crisis is not abstract — it is felt daily by hundreds of thousands of residents. Boghi Dushanbe addresses this directly: 12,000–15,000 new homes, modern schools, clinics, and parks, all within a 15-minute walkable neighborhood framework.

This is not development for its own sake. It is development in service of the people of Dushanbe and the Republic of Tajikistan.

Site Characteristics

  • Size & shape: Approximately 30 hectares (295,000–300,000 sqm) with a roughly rectangular configuration and a perimeter of approximately 2,792 meters, providing efficient buildable area with strong edge frontage.
  • Current use: Industrial and thermal power infrastructure, likely associated with Dushanbe's CHP (combined heat and power) network. Active operations would need to be phased out or relocated as part of any redevelopment.
  • Location: Luchob area, central Dushanbe. Proximate to the city's botanical gardens, within the urban core, and positioned along what may be a significant road or infrastructure corridor.
  • Topography: Dushanbe sits in the Hissar Valley at approximately 800m elevation, with the Fann Mountains visible to the north. The site is likely gently sloped, following the valley floor gradient.
  • River proximity: The Varzob River runs through central Dushanbe. Depending on exact site boundaries, there may be opportunity for waterfront development or at minimum visual connection to the river corridor.

Context & Adjacency

  • Botanical gardens: The proximity to Dushanbe's botanical gardens creates a landscape narrative — Boghi Dushanbe as an extension of the city's green infrastructure.
  • Road access: Central Dushanbe has a grid of major arterials. The site likely fronts onto at least one primary road, with potential for multiple vehicular access points along its 2.8km perimeter.
  • Institutional proximity: Central Dushanbe houses government ministries, embassies, universities, and the National Museum. This adjacency enhances commercial viability and civic significance.
  • Mountain views: The Fann Mountains and surrounding ranges provide dramatic visual backdrops. Building orientation and height graduation should preserve view corridors.
  • Gateway potential: A 30-hectare district in central Dushanbe would be visible and accessible from major city approaches, functioning as both a destination and a landmark.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Exceptionally large contiguous site in city center
  • Proximity to civic, cultural, and institutional uses
  • Strong mountain and potential river views
  • Adjacency to botanical gardens supports green branding
  • Existing road infrastructure on perimeter
  • Scale enables holistic master planning
  • Single-ownership (likely state) simplifies land assembly

Weaknesses

  • Active industrial operations require relocation
  • Potential soil contamination from decades of industrial use
  • Infrastructure (power, water, sewage) may need wholesale replacement
  • Limited precedent for large-scale mixed-use development in Tajikistan
  • Decommissioning timeline uncertain
  • Existing structures may have limited adaptive reuse potential

Opportunities

  • Addresses critical housing shortage directly
  • Aligns with World Bank and EBRD urban development strategies
  • City expansion plan to 2040 creates policy support
  • Real estate market growing at 4.69% annually
  • Rental yields of ~10% attract investor interest
  • National modernization narrative (RMJM Tower, infrastructure investment)
  • Potential for PPP structures with international developers
  • Tourism growth potential (Silk Road, mountain tourism)

Threats

  • Environmental remediation costs may be significant
  • Political and regulatory complexity
  • Financing constraints in a developing economy
  • Construction capacity and supply chain limitations
  • Currency and macroeconomic risk
  • Public opposition to industrial facility relocation
  • Regional geopolitical uncertainty

05 — Master Plan Concept

Two Paths Forward

We present two development schemes — one pragmatic, one aspirational — to illustrate the range of possibilities. Either can be refined, or elements combined, based on government priorities and market conditions.

Pragmatic

Scheme A

"The Balanced City"

A human-scale, livable district organized around a central green spine. This scheme prioritizes residential delivery, neighborhood completeness, and phased economic viability.

Land Use Allocation

Residential (towers + mid-rise)60%
Commercial / Office15%

Character Zones

  • Northern Residential Quarter: 8–12 story residential towers with ground-floor retail, organized around shared courtyards with mature tree planting. Family-oriented units of 80–140 sqm.
  • Central Green Spine: A 30-meter-wide landscaped promenade running east-west, connecting the botanical gardens edge to the district's commercial heart. Water channels, seating, and children's play areas.
  • Retail High Street: A 600-meter pedestrian-priority avenue with ground-floor shops, cafes, and restaurants. Mid-rise apartments above (6–8 stories). Character inspired by European market streets adapted to Tajik climate.
  • Office Cluster: Three to four 12–18 story Class A office buildings near the primary road frontage, providing modern commercial space for domestic and international firms.
  • Community Core: Central mosque (capacity 2,000+), primary school, medical clinic, and public library grouped around a civic plaza.
  • Southern Residential & Park: Mid-rise residential (6–10 stories) surrounding a 3-hectare neighborhood park with sports facilities, amphitheater, and mountain-view terraces.

Density & Character

Average FAR of 3.5–4.0 across the site. Maximum building height of 80 meters (office cluster). Predominantly a mid-rise, walkable neighborhood with generous public realm. Parking primarily below-grade.

Ambitious

Scheme B

"The Landmark District"

A signature urban destination that creates a new skyline for Dushanbe. This scheme concentrates density and ambition to produce iconic architecture and a nationally significant destination.

Residential (including luxury)45%

Character Zones

  • Landmark Tower (200m+): A 50–60 story mixed-use tower at the district's apex, housing a 5-star hotel (upper floors), Grade A offices (mid-levels), and an observation deck with panoramic mountain views. Designed by an internationally renowned architect to become Dushanbe's defining silhouette.
  • Cultural Center & Amphitheater: A 15,000 sqm performing arts and exhibition center with a 1,500-seat main hall, exhibition galleries, and an outdoor amphitheater. Architecture that reinterprets the Tajik "chaikhona" (teahouse) tradition at monumental scale.
  • Destination Mall & Entertainment: A 120,000 sqm lifestyle mall with international and local retail, multiplex cinema, indoor ice rink, and food halls. Designed as an architectural attraction, not a box.
  • Tech Innovation Hub: A cluster of flexible office and co-working buildings oriented toward technology, fintech, and creative industries, with a startup incubator and conference facilities.
  • Waterfront Promenade: If river proximity allows, a 1.2km landscaped riverwalk with restaurants, pop-up markets, and recreational areas. If not river-adjacent, a linear water feature with fountains and canal-like elements.
  • Luxury Residential Enclave: Premium tower residences (25–40 stories) with mountain views, private amenities, concierge services, and branded residences potential (Aman, Four Seasons, etc.).

Density & Character

Average FAR of 5.0–6.0 across the site, with peak FAR of 12+ at the tower cluster. Maximum height 200m+ (landmark tower). A dramatic, high-density skyline district. International architectural competition for key buildings.

Recommendation: We suggest beginning with Scheme A as the foundation — delivering the housing, schools, parks, and retail that Dushanbe needs most urgently — while reserving the southern or eastern portion of the site for Scheme B elements (landmark tower, cultural center, destination retail) to be phased in as market conditions and financing allow. This hybrid approach balances pragmatism with ambition.
06 — Design Language

Contemporary Central Asian Urbanism

The architectural identity of Boghi Dushanbe must honor Tajikistan's extraordinary artistic heritage while asserting a thoroughly contemporary vision. The design language bridges tradition and modernity without pastiche or kitsch.

Facade Language

Clean, geometric forms with depth and texture. Contemporary reinterpretations of mashrabiya screens — the perforated geometric screens of Islamic architecture — rendered in anodized aluminum, weathering steel, or precast concrete. These screens provide solar shading (critical in Dushanbe's continental climate with hot summers), visual privacy in residential buildings, and a rhythmic, human-scale pattern language that connects to centuries of Central Asian decorative art.

The geometric patterns draw specifically from Ismaili and broader Islamic geometric traditions prevalent in Tajikistan — interlocking stars, hexagonal tessellations, and arabesques — abstracted to contemporary precision rather than literal reproduction.

Materials Palette

Travertine: Locally sourced from Tajikistan's extensive limestone deposits. Used for podium cladding, public realm paving, and feature walls. Warm cream and honey tones that glow in Dushanbe's clear mountain light.

Local granite: Dark grey-black granite for base courses, plaza paving, and water feature edges. Provides gravitas and permanence.

Anodized aluminum: Bronze and champagne-toned anodized aluminum for mashrabiya screens, balcony railings, and tower crowns. Low maintenance, corrosion-resistant, and capable of precision geometric fabrication.

Glass curtain walls: High-performance glazing for office and tower buildings. Low-emissivity coatings for energy efficiency, with selective use of fritted or etched glass incorporating geometric patterns.

Skyline Composition

Heights graduated from the edges to the center or key node, creating a legible silhouette from multiple city viewpoints. The landmark tower anchors the composition, with surrounding buildings stepping down in deliberate increments. No building blocks the mountain view corridors identified in the master plan.

The skyline reads as a mountain range in miniature — peaks, ridges, and valleys — an architectural metaphor for the Fann Mountains that frame Dushanbe's horizon. This is achieved through varied tower heights (80m, 120m, 150m, 200m+) and articulated rooflines rather than flat-topped uniformity.

Landscape & Public Realm

Mature tree planting: London plane trees, walnuts, mulberries, and elms (all native or naturalized in Tajikistan) planted at semi-mature size to create immediate canopy. The "bogh" (garden) identity demands lush, generous planting from day one.

Water features: Reflecting pools, channels (jui), and fountains referencing the Persian-Tajik garden tradition of flowing water as both cooling element and spiritual metaphor. Recycled water systems for sustainability.

Mountain-view corridors: East-west visual axes maintained through building placement, ensuring residents and visitors always feel connected to the Fann Mountains and the dramatic topography that defines Dushanbe's identity.

Night Identity

Warm gold uplighting on key facade elements and public buildings, creating a cohesive nocturnal identity visible from across the city. The landmark tower's crown illuminated with a constellation-inspired LED pattern — referencing Tajikistan's crown and stars on the national flag.

Retail promenades lit with festoon-style warm white lighting. Landscape features with embedded ground lighting in pools and channels. The overall effect: warm, inviting, and distinctly "golden" — a district that glows rather than glares.

Tajik Heritage Integration

Suzani motifs: The vibrant embroidered textiles of Tajikistan — suzani — abstracted into large-scale facade panels, public art installations, and paving patterns. Not literal reproduction, but the flowing botanical forms and radial symmetries of suzani translated into contemporary materials.

Chaikhona tradition: The Tajik teahouse — a place of community gathering, elevated on a platform with carpets and low tables — inspires the design of public pavilions, rooftop terraces, and community spaces throughout the district. These are social infrastructure, not decoration.

Islamic geometry: Girih tiles, muqarnas-inspired canopies, and geometric tessellations as functional architectural elements (shading, ventilation, wayfinding) rather than applied ornament.

09 — Economic & Strategic Value

The Case for Investment

◆

Job Creation

Construction phase: 20,000+ direct jobs over 10–15 years, including skilled trades, engineering, and project management. Permanent employment: 15,000–20,000 jobs in offices, retail, hospitality, education, healthcare, and facility management. Indirect employment multiplier of 2.5–3x creates an additional 40,000–60,000 jobs in the broader economy through supply chain effects.

◆

Tax Revenue

The completed district would generate significant municipal and national tax revenue through property taxes, business taxes, VAT on retail and hospitality, income taxes from employees, and land value capture mechanisms. At maturity, the district could contribute meaningfully to Dushanbe's annual municipal budget, reducing the city's dependence on central government transfers.

◆

Real Estate Value

At current Dushanbe market rates and projected appreciation (4.69% annual growth), the completed Boghi Dushanbe district could represent $3–5 billion in real estate value at maturity. This would make it the single most valuable real estate asset in Tajikistan and a cornerstone of national wealth. Rental yields of approximately 10% in the current market suggest strong investor returns.

◆

Tourism & Destination Appeal

The 5-star hotel, cultural center, destination retail, and architectural landmarks would position Boghi Dushanbe as a regional tourist destination. Dushanbe is increasingly recognized as a gateway to the Pamir Highway and Tajikistan's spectacular mountain landscapes. A world-class urban district enhances the city's appeal for business tourism, cultural tourism, and stopover visitors.

◆

Foreign Investment

A well-structured PPP (public-private partnership) for Boghi Dushanbe could attract investment from international development finance institutions (EBRD, IFC, ADB), sovereign wealth funds (particularly Gulf-based funds with interest in Central Asia), and international real estate developers. The project's scale and government backing provide the credibility that institutional investors require.

◆

National Prestige & City Branding

Boghi Dushanbe would become Tajikistan's most visible urban development project, comparable in ambition to Astana's new city center (Kazakhstan) or Ashgabat's marble-clad modernization (Turkmenistan), but with a more humane, livable, and economically sustainable approach. International media coverage of a well-designed Central Asian district project would enhance Tajikistan's global reputation.

Catalytic Economic Effects

Large-scale urban redevelopment generates value far beyond the site boundary. International experience (London Docklands, Barcelona 22@, Songdo IBD) demonstrates that well-planned flagship districts increase property values in surrounding neighborhoods by 15–30% within the first decade.

For Dushanbe, Boghi Dushanbe would:

  • Stimulate upgrading of adjacent residential and commercial areas
  • Attract additional private investment to the Luchob area
  • Improve infrastructure (roads, utilities, public transport) that benefits the entire city
  • Create a demonstration effect for quality design and construction standards
  • Develop local workforce skills in modern construction and urban management

PPP Structure Logic

The optimal delivery mechanism is a public-private partnership where:

  • Government contributes: Land (via long-term lease or transfer), regulatory facilitation, infrastructure investment, and political commitment
  • Private sector provides: Development capital, construction expertise, management capability, and market risk absorption
  • DFIs participate: Concessional financing, technical assistance, environmental and social safeguards, and international credibility
  • Revenue sharing: Government receives land lease payments, tax revenue, and retained equity in key assets (e.g., cultural center, public realm)

This structure aligns with EBRD's stated preference for PPP models in its 2025–2030 Tajikistan strategy and with the World Bank's Dushanbe Sustainable Urban Development Project framework.

10 — Phasing Strategy

A Decade of Deliberate Transformation

Boghi Dushanbe is not built in a day. The phasing strategy prioritizes early visible wins, builds market confidence progressively, and reserves the most ambitious elements for when the district's success is proven.

Phase 0 — Foundation (Year 0–1)

Studies, Approvals & Stakeholder Alignment

Commission comprehensive feasibility study (market, financial, technical). Conduct environmental site assessment and remediation planning. Complete geotechnical and topographic surveys. Engage international master planning firm (competition or direct appointment). Initiate stakeholder consultation: government agencies, neighboring communities, potential investors. Secure cabinet-level approval and mayoral endorsement. Establish project delivery authority (dedicated SPV or government agency). Begin international marketing to potential investors and development partners.

Phase 1 — Genesis (Year 1–3)

Decommissioning, Infrastructure & First Deliveries

Commence industrial facility decommissioning and environmental remediation. Install primary infrastructure: roads, utilities, drainage, telecommunications. Quick Win #1: Open 3-hectare public park and green spine (first visible sign of transformation; generates community goodwill). Quick Win #2: Deliver first 2,000 residential units (mid-rise, family-oriented, affordably priced) to demonstrate livability. Quick Win #3: Open first 200m of retail high street with ground-floor cafes, bakeries, and convenience retail. Begin construction of primary school and medical clinic.

Phase 2 — Momentum (Year 3–6)

Office, Hotel, Expanded Residential & Cultural Anchor

Deliver first office cluster (2–3 buildings, 80,000–120,000 sqm). Open business hotel (200–250 keys). Expand residential to 6,000–8,000 cumulative units, including first premium towers. Complete retail high street (full 600m). Begin construction of cultural center and amphitheater. Open mosque and community facilities. Launch international architectural competition for landmark tower. District population reaches 15,000–20,000 residents.

Phase 3 — Landmark (Year 6–10)

Landmark Tower, Destination Retail & Full District Identity

Construct landmark tower (200m+) including 5-star hotel and observation deck. Open destination mall and entertainment complex. Complete tech innovation hub. Deliver cumulative 10,000–12,000 residential units. Open cultural center for full programming. The district achieves critical mass and self-sustaining commercial viability. Dushanbe's skyline is permanently transformed.

Phase 4 — Maturation (Year 10–15)

Remaining Parcels & Long-Term Value Creation

Develop remaining residential and commercial parcels based on demonstrated demand. Fine-tune public realm: additional landscaping, public art program, seasonal programming. Introduce branded residences and luxury segment if market supports. Complete all infrastructure and parking. District reaches full population capacity of 30,000–45,000. Boghi Dushanbe is established as the premier address in Tajikistan.

11 — Government Approval Strategy

Framing the Public Interest

The success of Boghi Dushanbe depends on strong, sustained government commitment. The project must be framed not as a commercial real estate venture but as a national initiative that serves the people of Tajikistan.

Modernization of the Capital

Dushanbe is the face of Tajikistan to the world. As the nation's diplomatic, commercial, and cultural center, the capital's physical environment directly reflects national ambition and capability. Boghi Dushanbe transforms an industrial-era liability into a 21st-century asset, demonstrating that Tajikistan is building for the future while honoring its past.

Livability & Quality of Life

The project directly addresses the housing crisis that affects hundreds of thousands of Dushanbe residents. By delivering 12,000–15,000 modern homes alongside schools, clinics, parks, and retail, Boghi Dushanbe creates a complete neighborhood where families can thrive. This is government serving its citizens in the most tangible way possible.

Jobs & Economic Growth

With 20,000+ construction jobs and 15,000+ permanent positions, Boghi Dushanbe represents one of the largest job creation initiatives in Tajikistan's modern history. The project provides employment across skill levels, from laborers to engineers, from shop assistants to hotel managers, creating a broad-based economic benefit.

Environmental Upgrade

Replacing industrial infrastructure with green urbanism — parks, tree-lined streets, clean buildings, and modern waste management — is an environmental win for Dushanbe. The project eliminates a source of industrial pollution and creates the city's largest new green space, contributing to air quality, biodiversity, and climate resilience.

12 — Risk Assessment

Risks & Mitigation Strategies

A responsible proposal must acknowledge risks transparently. The following analysis identifies key risks and proposes practical mitigation measures.

Risk Impact Likelihood Description Mitigation Strategy
Land Ownership & Concession High Medium The site's ownership structure, existing concessions, and legal framework for transfer or long-term lease may be complex, involving multiple state entities. Commission legal and land tenure review as first priority. Engage the State Committee on Land Management early. Establish clear governmental authority over the project. Consider special economic zone or development zone designation.
Industrial Operations Relocation High High Active thermal power and industrial operations cannot be abruptly terminated. Relocation or replacement of energy infrastructure requires careful planning to avoid disruption to city services. Phase development to allow continued operations during transition. Coordinate with Barqi Tojik (national energy company) on replacement facilities. Incorporate transition costs into project budget. Allow 2–3 year decommissioning timeline.
Environmental Remediation High High Decades of industrial activity may have contaminated soil and groundwater. Remediation costs can be significant and timelines unpredictable. Conduct Phase I and Phase II environmental assessments before any commitment. Budget conservatively for remediation (5–10% of total project cost). Engage international environmental engineering firm. Explore EBRD/World Bank environmental remediation funding.
Financing Complexity High Medium A $3–5B project requires multi-source financing over 15 years. Tajikistan's sovereign credit rating and currency risk may deter some investors. Phase investment to match market absorption. Structure PPP to share risk appropriately. Engage DFIs (EBRD, IFC, ADB) for anchor financing and credibility. Consider Somoni-denominated revenue streams for residential components. Diversify investor base (Gulf SWFs, Chinese investors, Turkish developers).
Public Concerns Medium Medium Residents may have concerns about displacement, construction disruption, affordability of new housing, or loss of familiar landmarks. Early and transparent public consultation. Commit to affordable housing component (minimum 30% of residential units at below-market rates). Deliver public park and community facilities first. Establish community liaison office. Provide employment priority to local residents.
Traffic & Infrastructure Strain Medium Medium Adding 30,000–45,000 residents and 15,000–20,000 workers will significantly increase traffic on surrounding roads and demand on utilities. Commission traffic impact study early. Design internal road network to distribute traffic to multiple access points. Invest in road upgrades as part of Phase 1 infrastructure. Design for future public transport integration (BRT or light rail). Mandate below-grade parking to keep streets pedestrian-friendly.
Political & Regulatory Coordination Medium Low The project spans multiple government jurisdictions and requires sustained political commitment across electoral cycles. Establish project at the highest level of government authority. Create dedicated project delivery authority with clear mandate. Develop cross-party support by emphasizing public benefits. Embed the project in the city's 2040 master plan. Secure early, formal approvals that create institutional momentum.
Construction Capacity Medium Medium Tajikistan's construction industry may lack the capacity and technical skills for a project of this scale and quality. Engage international contractors for key buildings (tower, hotel, cultural center). Invest in workforce training programs. Phase construction to allow capacity building. Partner with Turkish, Chinese, or Gulf construction firms with Central Asian experience.
13 — Closing Argument

Cities Are Built by Those Who Dare to Imagine Them

Every great city has a moment when it decides to become something more. For Paris, it was Haussmann's boulevards. For Singapore, it was the transformation of a swamp into Marina Bay. For Astana, it was the audacious decision to build a new capital on the steppe. These were not inevitable outcomes — they were acts of vision, courage, and sustained commitment.

Dushanbe stands at such a moment. The 30 hectares at the heart of this proposal are not merely land — they are a canvas for national ambition. The industrial infrastructure that occupies the site served Tajikistan in an earlier era. What replaces it can serve Tajikistan for the next century: homes for families, schools for children, parks for communities, workplaces for a new generation, and landmarks that tell the world Tajikistan is building a future worthy of its extraordinary people and landscape.

Boghi Dushanbe is not a fantasy. It is a disciplined, phased, financially structured proposal that learns from international best practice while remaining rooted in Tajik culture, climate, and values. It addresses the housing crisis. It creates tens of thousands of jobs. It attracts international investment. It transforms the city's skyline and its citizens' quality of life.

But it begins with a decision. A decision to commission the feasibility studies that turn vision into evidence. A decision to engage the international expertise that turns ambition into architecture. A decision to trust that Tajikistan deserves world-class urbanism — and can deliver it.

We respectfully recommend that the Government of the Republic of Tajikistan and the Mayor of Dushanbe authorize a comprehensive feasibility study for the Boghi Dushanbe project, encompassing market analysis, environmental assessment, infrastructure evaluation, financial structuring, and preliminary master planning. The garden is ready to be planted. The question is not whether Dushanbe deserves this — it is whether this generation will have the vision to build it.
